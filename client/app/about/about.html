About

In June 2015, a small team from The Times and Sunday Times competed and won at GEN + BBC News Labs #newsHACK. We created a concept called The Journalist Toolbox, proposing that newsroom tools needed to be more accessible for journalists, and standardised to create better journalism.

That would level the playing field between large news organisations and individual journalists, while also inspiring a higher overall quality of newsroom tools.

The prize was a partnership with the BBC and Goldsmiths, University of London, to turn the concept into reality.

The Journalist Toolbox is an open-source, online resource collecting tried-and-tested tools for journalists producing digital news.

Here, journalists can easily discover tools that can help them tell their stories better, while developers can showcase their new tools and projects.

We want this to be a space that will seamlessly bring journalists and developers together, while providing journalists a simple platform to explore, experiment, and implement new tools.

You can help make this site even more useful by giving feedback on the tools based on your experience using them. You can do this by up- or down-voting the tools and contribute reviews, which will help to inform the quality and relevance of each listed tool over time.

You will also be able to create and store private lists based on your favourite, most-frequently-used tools, and share and embed these lists, similar to playlists on Spotify and Pandora.

The Journalist Toolbox is a work in progress, as we constantly fine-tune it while new tools are added and community sharing grows.
